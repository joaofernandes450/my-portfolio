Personal Choice

Since I had my first contact with web development and consequently with the technologies represented, around my third year of graduation to be precise, that I have choosen to stick with them in both academic and personal projects.

Angular was and remains a simple but rather strong framework that allows me to design and develop all of my SPAs in the best way possible. With the introduction of Angular Material and its strong ongoing development, with new versions being pushed regurlary, that I see no motives to move on.

Node.js was at the time the chosen backend framework lectured and paired with Angular in our web development. Thanks to its flexibility, scalability and with NPM around I feel that Node.js is the best all around option for my backend development.

MongoDB at the time represented a new concept of mechanism of storage and retrieval of data, since it wasn't based on the common relational databases. But soon enough I found how powerful and fast MongoDB was. Plus the ease of communication between MongoDB and Node.js, using documents with structure similar to JSON, is a big plus.
